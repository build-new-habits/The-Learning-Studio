# The Learning Studio
## Digital Learning Pyramid Resource Hub — Product Blueprint v6
17/07/26 v6 | Synthesis: positioning, design language, and the v5 review fixes | Build brief for Gemini + GitHub

---

## 1. Executive Summary — v6 Position

v6 does not introduce new scope. It closes the gap between "safely in your lane" and "insanely useful" by making explicit something that has been true since v1 but never stated cleanly: digital practice exists in service of teaching, learning and assessment — it strengthens how staff deliver TLA, without ever defining what that TLA practice should be. Section 3 states this as a formal position, not an aside.

Everything flagged in the last review is fixed here: four staff-situation rows reframed from pedagogy to digital tools, one row cut outright, the WalkThrus reference removed, framework alignment moved to a controlled vocabulary, an automated schema validator added to governance rather than relying on written rules alone, and all five V1 cards — not two — built to prove the discovery model.

This version also answers a question nobody had asked yet: what "beautiful" actually means here, in specifics a builder can implement, not adjectives a builder has to interpret. Section 10 gives Gemini an accessible, verified colour palette, a named accessible typeface, and concrete layout rules — because "calm and modern" was never buildable, and this project's own governance rule is that accessibility must be tested, not eyeballed. That rule now applies to beauty as well.

## 3. Position: Digital Support for TLA, Not TLA Ownership

**This is the sentence that resolves the tension in every review so far, and it should be reproduced wherever the product is described to anyone outside the immediate build team.**

| The Learning Studio does | The Learning Studio does not |
|---|---|
| Strengthen how staff deliver existing teaching, learning and assessment practice, through digital, accessible and inclusive means | Define, prescribe or judge what that teaching, learning or assessment practice should be |
| Translate a real staff situation into a digital possibility and an accessible resource | Translate a real staff situation into a pedagogical instruction or assessment method |
| Reference established frameworks (Rosenshine, UDL, QFT) to explain why a digital practice works | Claim ownership, authorship or institutional endorsement of those frameworks |
| Make the case, through demonstrated usefulness, for what a wider platform could become | Propose, launch or govern that wider platform without separate institutional sponsorship |

> Every future card and Finder route can be tested against this table in seconds: if the answer to a staff situation is a tool, a setting, a template or an accessible format, it belongs here. If the answer is a teaching decision, an assessment judgement, or an evidence methodology, it belongs to someone else.

## 5. Authority and Scope Boundary

| In scope — current digital remit | Out of scope — separate ownership |
|---|---|
| Digital Foundations, Digital Inclusion and Digital Innovation content, guidance and resource routing, in service of existing TLA delivery | Defining, prescribing or judging whole-college teaching, learning or assessment strategy |
| Situation-first discovery from real staff language to digital, accessible resources | Any staff capability self-assessment — Jisc Discovery's role, not duplicated here |
| Reference-only alignment to public frameworks (Rosenshine, UDL, QFT, DigCompEdu, WCAG) | Any commercial or trademarked framework product whose value is a specific format (e.g. WalkThrus) — reference the underlying concept via its open source instead |
| Aggregate, non-identifying usefulness signals and dated, reviewed content | Any capture, storage or export of individual staff or learner data |
| Demonstrating a reusable pattern that could inform a future institutionally owned platform | Owning, proposing or pre-architecting that wider platform without separate sponsorship |

## 7. Problem-to-Practice Discovery Architecture

| Stage | What the user sees | What the system does |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Staff situation | "What are you trying to do?" | Captures the problem in teacher language |
| 2. Underlying digital need | Lightly shown as "You might be trying to…" | Maps the situation to a digital capability gap — never a pedagogical diagnosis |
| 3. Digital possibilities | "Here are some ways digital could help." | Suggests no-tech, low-tech, digital and AI-supported routes |
| 4. Digital Practice Card | A polished, shareable card, built to the Section 10 design spec | Steps, why it works, accessibility and inclusion lenses, framework alignment, downloads |
| 5. Resource action | Download, watch, search, try, adapt | Moves staff into action without needing the taxonomy first |

## 8. Refined Staff Situation Bank

| Staff question | Underlying digital need | Digital discovery routes |
|---|---|---|
| How do I catch up absent learners? | Capture and share learning digitally for later access | Teams recap post; photo capture; short screen/audio explanation; accessible summary; retrieval questions from lesson notes |
| How do I show learners what I'm thinking? | Make expert thinking visible and reusable | Visualiser; screen recording; OneNote annotation; annotated slides; short think-aloud video |
| How do I capture live modelling? | Record and reuse a demonstration digitally | Document camera/visualiser; phone/tablet video; Stream; Clipchamp; Teams post |
| How do I run a quick digital check for understanding? | A fast, low-friction digital capture of responses | Mini whiteboards; Plickers; Forms; polls; exit ticket; photo/voice evidence |
| How do I give learners digital tools to work more independently? | Reduce reliance on repeated live explanation through reusable digital resources | Checklists; how-to videos; Teams channels; structured FAQs; AI-generated learner checklists |
| How do I support reading and writing barriers? | Digital access and assistive technology for text | Immersive Reader; Read&Write; dictation; captions; text-to-speech; accessible Word layout |
| How do I make resources accessible? | WCAG-aware structure and assistive-technology compatibility | Headings; alt text; captions; colour contrast; Accessibility Checker; accessible templates |
| How do I reduce repeated explanations? | Reusable digital explanation that learners can return to | Short videos; Teams FAQs; recap posts; annotated screenshots; step-by-step guides |
| How do I capture practical work digitally? | Digital capture of a process or product for later reference | Photo evidence; video snippets; Teams assignment upload; voice notes |

> Note: "How do I evidence practical work without overcomplicating it?" was deliberately removed from the v5 draft — digital capture of practical work is in scope; judging what counts as sufficient assessment evidence is not.

## 9. Platform-Wide Learning Lenses

| Lens | Boundary |
|---|---|

| UDL | Supporting lens, not a full UDL training programme |

| Evidence-informed | References Rosenshine, UDL or other open, public-domain research only. No trademarked formats, no proprietary card systems |

## 10. Design Language — Beautiful and Accessible by Construction

Accessibility is tested, not eyeballed — the same rule applies to visual design. Every value below is buildable and verifiable, not descriptive.

### Colour palette (contrast calculated against WCAG 2.1/2.2 formulae)

| Role | Hex | Use | Verified contrast |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ink | #1F4B57 | Body text, headings, on white/light backgrounds | 9.54:1 on white — exceeds AAA |
| Action | #0F7A6B | Buttons, links, active states — white text on top | 5.23:1 white-on-accent — passes AA |
| Surface | #F4F7F6 | Card and section backgrounds — never used for text itself | N/A — background only |

> Every colour combination actually used in the build must still be tested against its real background with a contrast tool before release — this palette is the starting point, not a substitute for that gate.

### Layout and interaction rules

| Element | Specification |
|---|---|
| Typeface | Atkinson Hyperlegible — open-licence, purpose-built for reading clarity and accessibility, free to use |
| Line length | 65–75 characters per line for body text |
| Touch targets | Minimum 44×44px for every interactive element, per WCAG 2.2 target-size guidance |
| Motion | Respects prefers-reduced-motion at the system level by default. No animation carries meaning on its own |
| Card surfaces | Flat colour only — Surface (#F4F7F6) or white. No semi-transparent overlays (these pass automated contrast checks while failing real screen-reader/low-vision use) |
| Focus states | Visible focus outline distinct from hover state, tested against every background it appears on |
| Imagery | Original diagrams or icons only. No stock photography |

## 11. Digital Practice Cards

| Component | Specification |
|---|---|
| Card title | Plain language, situation before tool name — e.g. "Show your thinking live with a visualiser" |
| Why this works | 40–60 words, plain language |
| Try this | 3–5 steps, simplest viable approach first |
| Routes | No-tech, low-tech, digital, AI-supported, where each genuinely applies |
| Learning lenses | Accessibility and inclusion always present; UDL, trauma-informed and evidence-informed only where genuinely relevant |
| Framework alignment | Selected from the controlled vocabulary in Section 14's schema — never freeform text |
| Source and review | Source area, last-reviewed date, review status. No named individual required for V1 |
| Downloads | Word guide or checklist, reusing existing Word-source material where it exists |

## 13. Gemini + GitHub Build Model

| Layer | Detail |
|---|---|
| Source of truth | GitHub repo — code, schemas, content, version history, issues, handoff records |
| Site Manager | A Gemini Gem maintaining the repo map, master schedule and issue log |
| Builder sessions | Scoped to files provided in-session only; never assume a file exists or does not exist |
| Automated validation | A committed script (Node + a JSON schema validator such as ajv) runs on every commit: validates every card and route against the schema files, and hard-fails if any related_cards/suggested_cards ID does not resolve to a real file |
| Human review | Graeme reviews content quality and Weston-specific wording for V1 |

### AI Builder Rules (non-negotiable)
- Never guess file structure, content, or intent — ask if something is missing
- Never edit a file that hasn't been provided or opened in the current session
- Every file and content item starts with a version header: DD/MM/YY v# 
- No personal data fields anywhere — code, schema, forms, or local storage
- The Finder must never ask users to self-rate confidence, skill or capability
- Framework alignment values must come from the pre-approved vocabulary list only
- All colour/contrast choices must be tested with a real tool, not eyeballed
- Every session ends with a handoff: files changed, reason, tests run, assumptions, unresolved risks, next action

## 15. QA Gates

1. **Scope alignment** — every output passes the Section 3 test
2. **No Jisc duplication** — the Finder never asks users to rate confidence, skill or capability
3. **Schema validation** — automated script passes on every commit
4. **Accessibility** — WCAG 2.1/2.2 AA, keyboard nav, focus states, contrast tested with a real tool, 200% zoom, 44×44px touch targets
5. **Design language** — Section 10 palette, typeface and layout rules followed and verified
6. **Inclusion lenses** — every card includes accessibility and inclusion considerations at minimum
7. **Framework safety** — no trademarked or commercial framework formats referenced
8. **Handoff quality** — files changed, tests run, assumptions, unresolved risks, next action, every session
