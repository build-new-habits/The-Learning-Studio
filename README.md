# The Learning Studio

## 3. Position: Digital Support for TLA, Not TLA Ownership
This is the sentence that resolves the tension in every review so far, and it should be reproduced wherever the product is described to anyone outside the immediate build team.

| The Learning Studio does | The Learning Studio does not |
|---|---|
| Strengthen how staff deliver existing teaching, learning and assessment practice, through digital, accessible and inclusive means | Define, prescribe or judge what that teaching, learning or assessment practice should be |
| Translate a real staff situation into a digital possibility and an accessible resource | Translate a real staff situation into a pedagogical instruction or assessment method |
| Reference established frameworks (Rosenshine, UDL, QFT) to explain why a digital practice works | Claim ownership, authorship or institutional endorsement of those frameworks |
| Make the case, through demonstrated usefulness, for what a wider platform could become | Propose, launch or govern that wider platform without separate institutional sponsorship |

Every future card and Finder route can be tested against this table in seconds: if the answer to a staff situation is a tool, a setting, a template or an accessible format, it belongs here. If the answer is a teaching decision, an assessment judgement, or an evidence methodology, it belongs to someone else.

## 5. Authority and Scope Boundary

| In scope — current digital remit | Out of scope — separate ownership |
|---|---|
| Digital Foundations, Digital Inclusion and Digital Innovation content, guidance and resource routing, in service of existing TLA delivery | Defining, prescribing or judging whole-college teaching, learning or assessment strategy |
| Situation-first discovery from real staff language to digital, accessible resources | Any staff capability self-assessment — Jisc Discovery's role, not duplicated here |
| Reference-only alignment to public frameworks (Rosenshine, UDL, QFT, DigCompEdu, WCAG) | Any commercial or trademarked framework product whose value is a specific format (e.g. WalkThrus) — reference the underlying concept via its open source instead |
| Aggregate, non-identifying usefulness signals and dated, reviewed content | Any capture, storage or export of individual staff or learner data |
| Demonstrating a reusable pattern that could inform a future institutionally owned platform | Owning, proposing or pre-architecting that wider platform without separate sponsorship |
